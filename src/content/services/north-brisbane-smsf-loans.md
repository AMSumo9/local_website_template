## SMSF Property Loans North Brisbane

Use your self-managed super fund to invest in North Brisbane property. SMSF loans allow your super to borrow for residential or commercial property investment, building wealth for retirement while accessing tax advantages and property growth in high-performing North Brisbane suburbs.

### How SMSF Property Loans Work

**Limited Recourse Borrowing Arrangement (LRBA):**
SMSF legislation requires "limited recourse" structures. If the SMSF defaults, lenders can only claim the specific property purchased—not other SMSF assets. This protects your super fund's other holdings.

**Bare Trust Structure:**
Property is held in a bare trust (separate legal entity) until the loan is repaid. The SMSF is the beneficial owner and receives all rental income, but legal ownership transfers only when the loan is fully repaid. We arrange compliant structures through SMSF-specialist solicitors.

**Single Asset Rule:**
Each LRBA can only acquire one single acquirable asset. You cannot purchase multiple properties under one SMSF loan—each property requires separate borrowing arrangements and bare trusts.

### SMSF Loan Requirements

**Higher Deposits:**
SMSF lenders typically require 30-40% deposit (60-70% LVR maximum). This reflects higher risk compared to personal loans. Some specialist lenders offer up to 80% LVR but with significantly higher rates.

**Interest Rates:**
SMSF rates sit 1-2% above standard investment loan rates. Current indicative rates: 7.5-9.0% variable, 7.0-8.5% fixed. Larger funds with strong financials access better pricing.

**SMSF Compliance:**
Your SMSF must have compliant trust deed, investment strategy, and be administered by qualified SMSF administrator or accountant. We verify compliance before proceeding to avoid costly delays or rejections.

**Sufficient Fund Balance:**
Your SMSF needs sufficient cash for deposit, stamp duty, and transaction costs. Most lenders require 12 months of loan repayments held in reserve within the fund. For a $600k property, expect total cash requirement of $200-250k.

### Residential vs Commercial SMSF Property

**Residential Investment:**
North Brisbane houses, townhouses, or units leased to arm's length tenants. SMSF cannot lease residential property to fund members or related parties—strict prohibition. Popular SMSF suburbs: North Lakes ($720k median, 4.3% yield), Mango Hill ($640k, 4.7% yield), Caboolture ($610k, 5.0% yield).

**Commercial Property:**
Your SMSF can purchase commercial property and lease it to your own business. This is a powerful strategy—your business pays rent to your super fund, building retirement wealth while claiming rent as tax deduction. Popular for office, warehouse, retail premises in Chermside, Brendale, North Lakes business parks.

### Tax Benefits of SMSF Property Investment

**Rental Income Tax:**
Rental income received by SMSF is taxed at 15% (accumulation phase) or 0% (pension phase). Compare to personal tax rates of 32.5-45% for most investors—significant tax savings.

**Capital Gains Tax:**
If held 12+ months, capital gains taxed at 10% (accumulation phase) or 0% (pension phase). Personal capital gains tax can reach 22.5% for high earners. Long-term SMSF property investment is highly tax-efficient.

**Depreciation Claims:**
SMSF can claim building depreciation (2.5% annually for post-1985 properties) and plant & equipment depreciation. Quantity surveyor reports identify claimable depreciation, reducing SMSF taxable income.

### SMSF Investment Strategy

**Diversification:**
While property can be part of SMSF investment strategy, ensure diversification across asset classes. Sole purpose test requires SMSF investments to be made solely for retirement benefit—not personal use or enjoyment.

**Liquidity Management:**
Property is illiquid. Ensure your SMSF maintains sufficient cash reserves for operating expenses, insurance, maintenance, and member benefits. Most advisors recommend 10-20% cash allocation.

**Exit Strategy:**
Plan for eventual sale or transfer. When members retire and draw pensions, the property may need to be sold to provide liquidity for pension payments. Consider suburb selection based on future saleability.
